Archae

How are these different from eubacteria?

Eubacteria are considered “true bacteria”, and differences in their respective plasma membranes is what sets them apart. They are found in extreme thermal vents under conditions with no oxygen.




Fun Facts!

  1. One of the first discoveries is when scientists obtain samples from a stop deep in the Pacific ocean where molten rock and hot gasses are present.

  2. Another early discovery was by sampling the hot springs of Yellowstone National Park, USA.

  3. They produce biological methane.

  4. No species can perform photosynthesis.

  5. They do not produce spores, and are only capable of reproducing sexually.

  6. They have been found in oil wells.

  7. Types of archaea can use hydrogen, carbon dioxide, and ammonia to make organic matter.
